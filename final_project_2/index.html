History

Muay Thai is an ancient martial art and a national sport in Thailand.
It is also the most popular sport in South Asia surpassing other
sports disciplines such as Vietnamese boxing, Burmese boxing,
Laotian boxing,or even Khmer boxing.The history of Muay Thai is
intimately linked to that of Thailand.Also called Thai boxing,
Thai boxing, or Thai boxing,it was practiced for the first time
by Thai warriors to defend themselves and repel invaders,
especially the Burmese.

It must be recognized that the decision of King "Naresuan the Great"
to integrate Muay Thai into military training played an important role
in the promotion of this martial art. Over time, more and more Thais
learned Muay Thai, which contributed to the development of its techniques.
In the 18th century, the effectiveness of Muay Thai was demonstrated by the
famous Thai fighter"Nai Khanom Tom",who was captured by Burmese soldiers
and who managed to defeat all his opponents in a tournament played
in front of the king of Burma.Also in the 18th century,Muay Thai
became a national sport in Thailand following a decision by King
"Pra Chao Seua", who was nicknamed the Tiger King.

The development of tourism in the country, from the 20th century,
contributed to the export of Muay Thai all over the world, particularly
in the United States and Europe.Currently, Muay Thai is very popular
with young Europeans who move to Thailand to learn the techniques
of this martial art in our Sam Roi Yot training camp!
